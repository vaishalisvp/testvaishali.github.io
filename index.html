Hi, I’m Vaishali! I’m a data analyst currently working at ESMT Berlin. I work on exciting projects in the field of crowd-sourced social psychology, where I examine questions such as how to make psychological research more reliable, and how varying analytic choices affect research outcomes.

I graduated from the Hertie School of Governance with a Masters in Public Policy in the year 2018. I was a DAAD Helmut Schmidt scholar during this period, and my masters thesis examined the impact of parental separation on adolescent wellbeing in Germany. I also hold a Masters in Economics from the Indian Institute of Technology, Madras.

I work extensively with R, Python and SQL, and some of the projects I have worked on can be found here.

Beyond work, I enjoy playing competitive scrabble and trying different types of coffee (current favourite: Nachammai Estate - medium roast). I’m also passionate about functional fitness - anything from strength training, to hiking or kayaking when the weather’s good.
